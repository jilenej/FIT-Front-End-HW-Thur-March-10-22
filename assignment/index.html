Black Fashion Designers

Spring/Summer 22

View Slideshow

Sergio Hudson Ready-To-Wear S/S 2022

What would Whitley Gilbert from A Different World or Clueless’s Dionne Daveport (Dee) wear on safari? Well, they’d be characteristically overdressed, if you ask Sergio Hudson. “I grew up in the ’90s, and you’d watch these girls who were obsessed with fashion and just dress up for no reason,” he said backstage before the show. “That was what I was thinking about this collection.”.

Autumn Adeigbo Ready-To-Wear S/S 2022

Last year, Autumn Adeigbo made headlines as the 36th Black woman to raise $1 million in venture capital funding. She’s the first to do so for a fashion brand—a coup for several reasons, among them fashion’s history of exclusion and its generally risky business reputation. For spring 2022, Adeigbo pivoted from the clashing patterns of last season to a different kind of Over The Top Style: head-to-toe prints.

LaQuan Smith Ready-To-Wear S/S 2022

Location. Location. Location. During fashion month, the real estate a designer utilizes is almost as important as the clothes. Choosing a remarkable venue makes a statement, and LaQuan Smith unveiled his spring collection at New York’s iconic Empire State Building. As Art Deco masterpieces go, it doesn’t get much better, in transforming a vintage skyscraper into a modern event space. Smith’s show was designed and executed for the internet with camera drones that glided overhead catering to the online audience who have supported Smith from the start.
